An IT guy trying to make a better world by finding solutions to co-exist with natural disasters. Done flood risk assessment and Tsunami evacuation analysis for the disabled. Currently researching on volcanic ash disaster risk governance of the impending large-scale explosive eruptions. This includes modelling volcanic ash transport and dispersal simulations, building decision support systems, conducting disaster risk and impacts assessment, developing machine learning models, and performing anomaly analysis and extreme value analysis.

Experienced in various **programming languages** (*C, C++, C#, Java, Javascript, PHP, Ruby, Fortran, Golang*) and **scripting** (*PowerShell, Bash, C shell*) but now mostly do **Python, R, and Julia**. Love(d) to mingle with **databases** (*Oracle, MySQL, PostgreSQL, MSSQL, MongoDB, Elasticsearch, REDIS*), data warehouses, and **big data lakes** (*Redshift, Sparks, Hadoop*) on previous life. Oh, also play around **GIS** (*ArcGIS, QGIS, OSM*) and some **viz** tools (*Tableau, Origins*) too. When not busy researching, you can usually **find me** on top of mountains, football fields, cycling tracks, or behind lenses.
