An IT professional striving to create a better world by developing solutions to coexist with natural disasters. Currently working on developing flexible adaptation strategies to address future changes in hazards and society. Our focus is on advancing and integrating weather-related disaster and water resource modelling to project future extremes for climate disaster risk information. Experienced in flood risk analysis and management Tsunami evacuation mechanisms focusing on individuals with disabilities, and volcanic ash disaster risk governance of the impending large-scale explosive eruptions. My areas of expertise encompass the simulation of volcanic ash transport and dispersal models, development of decision support systems, analysis of dynamic decision-making under uncertainty, disaster risk and impact assessment, bias correction of machine learning models, development of advanced anomaly detection and extreme value analysis.

Has professional experiences in database (*Oracle, MySQL, PostgreSQL, MSSQL, MongoDB, Elasticsearch, REDIS*) / data lakes (*Redshift, Sparks, Hadoop*) administration and optimization, systems / site reliability engineering for variety of server-side architecture and cloud infrastructures (*AWS, GCP*), and software engineering with various **programming languages** (*C, C++, C#, Java, Javascript, PHP, Ruby, Fortran, Golang*), but now mostly do data science and machine learning (**Python, R, and Julia**). Love to mingle with Geographic Information Sciences and Data Visualization. Outside of research, we can all joyfully bond by hiking mountains, playing football, cycling, and capturing various photographs.
