An IT guy trying to make a better world by finding solutions to co-exist with natural disasters. Currently work on the flexible adaptation strategies to the future changes in hazards and society, focusing on advancing and integrating weather-related disaster and water resources modeling and projecting future extremes for climate disaster risk information. Used to analyse flood risk management and Tsunami evacuation mechanism for the disabled. Has been researching on volcanic ash disaster risk governance of the impending large-scale explosive eruptions. This includes modelling volcanic ash transport and dispersal simulations, building decision support systems, conducting disaster risk and impacts assessment, developing machine learning models, and performing anomaly analysis and extreme value analysis.

Experienced in various **programming languages** (*C, C++, C#, Java, Javascript, PHP, Ruby, Fortran, Golang*) and **scripting** (*PowerShell, Bash, C shell*) but now mostly do **Python, R, and Julia**. Love(d) to mingle with **databases** (*Oracle, MySQL, PostgreSQL, MSSQL, MongoDB, Elasticsearch, REDIS*), **big data lakes** (*Redshift, Sparks, Hadoop*), and **cloud architectures** (*AWS, GCP*) on previous life. Oh, also like to play around **GIS** (*ArcGIS, QGIS, OSM*) and some **viz** tools (*Tableau, Origins*) too. When not busy researching, you can usually **find me** on top of mountains, football fields, cycling tracks, or behind lenses.
